Overview

Purpose

The purpose of this site it to help inform those who are new to the miniature painting world. These webpages will help those who come to it by informing them the basics of painting, supplies they'll need, and other ways they can get informed about miniature gaming.

Audience

The main audiance of this project will be those who are new to miniature painting and looking to get into it. They'll be people who are interested getting into the hobby or already interested and looking for ways to help improve their painting stlyes. Also it will just be a great overview of miniature painting in general and to help inspire those who may not know what it is, to go and paint.

Home page

Miniature painting is a meditative hobby that has found itself in the hearts of many hobbyists around the globe. It has massive community that find themselves playing many different types of table top games with little plastic figures. The games look very involved and fun but you find the task of getting into the hobby a monumental task that will take many hours, dollars, and time to bear any fruits. You find that there are many people who are very welcoming and guide you in the right direction of where to get started. Upon going home, you search the internet for a step in the right direction and you soon find that there are so many different guides online spanning from YouTube videos to articles on the web. There are so many ways to start and it can be overwhelming, and that’s alright this is a step that everyone in the hobby has to go through at the beginning. This website is to help you get started and take a step in the right direction. We will explore the tools that you will need for the hobby and also the different ways to paint your miniatures (Yes you will have to paint some of the mini figures that you will get).

Getting Started

Important tools Miniatures: The first thing you'll need are the miniatures themselves. These can be plastic or resin figurines, depending on your preference and the models you want to paint. Paints: High-quality acrylic paints designed for miniatures are essential. Brands like Citadel, Vallejo, Army Painter, and Reaper Miniatures produce paints specifically for this hobby. These paints come in a wide range of colors. Paintbrushes: Invest in a set of high-quality paintbrushes designed for miniature painting. You'll want a variety of brush sizes for different tasks, including base coating, layering, and fine detailing. Look for brushes with fine tips and a good spring to maintain their shape. Palette: A palette, either a traditional one or a wet palette, is used to mix and thin your paints as you work. A wet palette helps keep your paints moist and usable for longer periods. Water or Thinner: You'll need water to clean your brushes and thin your paints when necessary. Some painters use acrylic paint thinners for certain techniques. Primer: Use a primer to prepare the miniature's surface for painting. Priming helps the paint adhere better and provides a consistent base color. Choose a color that complements your color scheme. Nippers: You will soon learn that your miniatures come on sprues and that the best way to get them off the sprue is using flat edged hobby nippers. They will make sure that your plastic model will come off nice and smoothly, you don’t want to cut off more of the model than needed to. Hobby Knife: A sharp hobby knife is handy for cleaning mold lines, removing unwanted material, and making minor modifications to the miniatures. Files and Sandpaper: These are used to smooth rough edges and remove imperfections on the miniature. Optional tools Magnification: Some painters use magnifying tools or magnifying glasses to help with fine details. Varnish: Matte or gloss varnish can be used to protect your finished work and give it a consistent look. Washes and Inks: These are used to add shading and depth to your miniatures. Palettes, Wet Palette Paper, or Pallet Pads: These are used for mixing and diluting paint. Holder or Stand: A miniature holder or stand can help you keep a steady hand while painting and prevent smudging. Remember that miniature painting is a highly customizable hobby, and you can add more tools and supplies as you become more experienced and develop your own preferences. It's important to have fun and enjoy the process of bringing your miniatures to life with your artistic touch.

Painting Techniques

Layering Miniature Painting Process: Layering is a technique used to build up smooth transitions of color, highlights, and shadows on a miniature. It is essential for achieving realistic and well-blended effects. Here's a step-by-step guide to the layering process: Materials Needed: Miniature Acrylic paints in the desired colors Brushes, including a detail brush Water or acrylic paint thinner (for thinning paints) Palette or wet palette Steps: Basecoat: Start with a clean and properly primed miniature. Apply the basecoat, which is the initial layer of paint, using the darkest color for the area you are working on. This serves as the foundation for your layering. Select Highlight Color: Choose a lighter color (a shade or two lighter than the basecoat) for your first layer of highlights. Thin the Paint: To create a smooth transition, thin down the highlight color with water or a paint medium. The paint should be semi-translucent, like heavy cream. Apply the First Layer: Load a small amount of thinned highlight paint on your brush. Apply this layer to the area where you want the first highlight, typically the raised portions or areas that catch more light. Blend the Edges: As you apply the thinned paint, blend the edges of the first layer into the basecoat. Use a gentle, controlled brushstroke to achieve a smooth transition. Feather the paint outwards so that it gradually fades into the basecoat. Wait for Drying: Allow this layer to dry completely before moving on to the next. This ensures that the layers won't mix and create a muddy appearance. Repeat the Process: Apply subsequent layers of highlights, each lighter than the last, in smaller and smaller areas. This gradually builds up the transition and intensity of the highlights. Continue blending the edges to ensure smooth transitions. Detail Work: Use a fine detail brush for the final, brightest highlights on the miniature. Apply these sparingly to the most prominent raised areas or where you want to draw the most attention. Shading: To add shadows, you can follow a similar process but use slightly darker colors for shading. Thin them down and apply them to the recessed areas and the areas you want to appear darker. Final Touches: Once you're satisfied with the layering, you can make final adjustments and details as needed. This might include adding accents, refining highlights, or correcting any imperfections. Dry Brushing Miniature Painting Process: Dry brushing is a technique used to highlight raised details and create texture on a miniature's surface. Here's a step-by-step guide to dry brushing: Materials Needed: Miniature Acrylic paints (a lighter color for dry brushing) Stiff, flat brush (often a "drybrush" brush) Paper towel or cloth Palette Steps: Preparation: Start with a clean and properly primed miniature. Loading the Brush: Dip the tip of the flat, stiff brush into the lighter paint color. Unlike other techniques, you don't need to thin the paint; instead, you want the brush to be almost dry. Removing Excess Paint: Wipe off most of the paint onto a paper towel or cloth. The goal is to have very little paint remaining on the brush. Dry Brushing: Gently and lightly brush the model's surface with the nearly dry brush. Hold the brush at a low angle to the surface and use a back-and-forth or circular motion. Focus on Raised Details: Dry brushing highlights the raised areas, edges, and textures on the miniature. The paint will catch on these raised parts, creating a natural highlight effect. Gradual Build-Up: It's crucial to build up the highlights gradually. After one pass, assess the results and decide if you want to add more layers of dry brushing. You can also vary the pressure to control the intensity of the effect. Texture and Details: Dry brushing is particularly effective for creating texture on surfaces like fur, stone, or chainmail. It can be used for terrain pieces or models that benefit from a weathered look. Cleanup: After dry brushing, you may need to touch up or clean up any areas where the paint strayed outside the intended highlight areas. Both layering and dry brushing are valuable techniques in the world of miniature painting. They serve different purposes, with layering being ideal for smooth transitions and blending, and dry brushing for creating texture and adding quick highlights. You can often use them in combination to achieve the desired effect on your miniatures. Practice and experimentation will help you master these techniques and produce stunning results.
