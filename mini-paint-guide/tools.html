Miniatures: The first thing you'll need are the miniatures themselves. These can be plastic or resin figurines, depending on your preference and the models you want to paint.

Paints: High-quality acrylic paints designed for miniatures are essential. Brands like Citadel, Vallejo, Army Painter, and Reaper Miniatures produce paints specifically for this hobby. These paints come in a wide range of colors.

Paintbrushes: Invest in a set of high-quality paintbrushes designed for miniature painting. You'll want a variety of brush sizes for different tasks, including base coating, layering, and fine detailing. Look for brushes with fine tips and a good spring to maintain their shape.

Palette: A palette, either a traditional one or a wet palette, is used to mix and thin your paints as you work. A wet palette helps keep your paints moist and usable for longer periods.

Water or Thinner: You'll need water to clean your brushes and thin your paints when necessary. Some painters use acrylic paint thinners for certain techniques.

Primer: Use a primer to prepare the miniature's surface for painting. Priming helps the paint adhere better and provides a consistent base color. Choose a color that complements your color scheme.

Hobby Knife: A sharp hobby knife is handy for cleaning mold lines, removing unwanted material, and making minor modifications to the miniatures.

Holder or Stand: A miniature holder or stand can help you keep a steady hand while painting and prevent smudging.
